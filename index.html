About

20+ years building production infrastructure and engineering teams.

My career started in 2007, but my love for it began earlier, the day I first saw Linux. From that moment on, I've tried to learn something new every day.

Across those years I've worked as a full-stack developer, DevOps engineer, platform engineer, and engineering manager, across 50+ companies. Different teams, different problems, the same instinct: keep learning, ship real things.

Somewhere in there, I also ran thousands of technical interviews. Enough to know exactly where the process breaks: candidates who ace LeetCode but freeze on day one, take-homes you can't trust, whiteboard puzzles that don't predict anything, and now AI everywhere, with no one quite sure how to interview around it.

What I'm building

EasyEnv: hire engineers in real environments.

EasyEnv is a technical hiring platform that runs live and take-home interviews on real Linux machines, in the browser. Candidates can use AI, just like in a real job. You watch every keystroke and see exactly how they think, debug, and ship.

Real environments

Production-grade Linux boxes, not sandboxed editors. Run anything the role actually requires.

AI allowed

Let candidates use AI the way they will at work. Process becomes the signal, not memorization.

Every keystroke

Full session replay across terminals, editor, and browser. Know how they got to the answer.
